about

My path to medicine was not linear and honestly that's the whole point.

I got into research because I thought it would look good on applications. I stayed because I accidentally fell in love with the intersection of computation and biology. One semester I was doing primate neurosurgery in the Bair Lab, running calcium imaging pipelines, and debugging Python at 2am. The next I was at Vanderbilt building protein design tools with AlphaFold and Rosetta. Somewhere in between I was doing CRISPR in the Hawn Lab trying to figure out how tuberculosis hijacks macrophages.

Outside the lab, I've spent over 1,600 hours as a medical assistant across cardiology and sleep medicine. I volunteer at Recovery Cafe, I train as a crisis counselor on Crisis Text Line, and I teach organic chemistry to students who think they're bad at it (they're not, the textbook is).

I also run The Overzealous Premed on YouTube because nobody told me what actually mattered when I started this journey. So I'm telling everyone else.

The through line? I like hard problems that sit between disciplines. The best stuff happens when you stop asking "what field is this?" and start asking "does this actually work?"

Bair Lab · University of Washington

Computational Neuroscience

I assist in non-human primate neurosurgeries and then analyze the data computationally. We're using two-photon and three-photon calcium imaging to study cross-orientation suppression in macaque visual cortex. I built the full XORI analysis pipeline: 4,785 ROIs across 28 cortical depths (140 to 518 microns), mixed-effects modeling that found a laminar shift from facilitation to suppression (p = 7.9 x 10^-10). Spatial frequency mediates ~40% of the variance.

first-author arXiv preprint · SfN 2025 poster · second-author manuscript in prep

LandmarkDiff

facial surgery prediction from a smartphone photo

A ControlNet-conditioned latent diffusion model that generates photorealistic before/after facial surgery previews from standard 2D clinical photos. No CT scanner required ($50K-$100K each). No published work does this with diffusion models and anatomical constraints. 530+ cataloged boundary conditions across mobile optics, 3D reconstruction, FLUX diffusion, biomechanics, and HPC infrastructure.

React Native FLUX.1-dev ControlNet FLAME FastAPI AWS/SLURM

Vision: Democratize surgical planning. Any patient, any clinic, any phone. What's needed: Clinical validation dataset, IRB approval, training compute on Hyak HPC.
