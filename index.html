flagship project

LandmarkDiff

Anatomically-Conditioned Latent Diffusion for Photorealistic Facial Surgery Outcome Prediction from Standard Clinical Photography

Current surgical planning tools require expensive 3D imaging — $50K–$100K per scanner. Every published approach operates on 3D point clouds or specialized X-ray imaging. No published work applies diffusion models to photorealistic surgical prediction from standard 2D clinical photos with anatomical constraints.

LandmarkDiff generates photorealistic before/after facial surgery previews from a single smartphone photo. The pipeline extracts 468 3D facial landmarks via MediaPipe, allows manipulation to encode a surgical plan, and uses a fine-tuned FLUX.1-dev diffusion model with ControlNet and IP-Adapter conditioning to generate post-operative predictions while preserving patient identity.

Multi-procedure: rhinoplasty, lip augmentation, jawline contouring, blepharoplasty, and more. 530+ cataloged boundary conditions. 200+ technical specification sections.
