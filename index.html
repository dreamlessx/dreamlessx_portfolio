about

My path to medicine was not linear and honestly that's the whole point.

I'm a junior at UW studying biochemistry and neuroscience. I got into research because I thought it would look good on applications. I stayed because I accidentally fell in love with building things that sit between computation and biology.

Right now I split my time between the Bair Lab where I do primate neurosurgery and calcium imaging analysis, and the Meiler Lab at Vanderbilt where I build protein design pipelines. Before that I was doing CRISPR in the Hawn Lab, molecular docking in the Anderson Lab, and neural network modeling in the Stiber Lab.

Outside the lab I'm a medical assistant, a crisis counselor, a YouTube creator, a volunteer, and a guy who probably has too many VS Code windows open. I teach organic chemistry to students who think they're bad at it. They're not. The textbook is just written by someone who forgot what it's like to learn.

I run The Overzealous Premed on YouTube because nobody told me what actually mattered when I started this journey. So I'm telling everyone else. No filter, no polish.

The through line is that I like hard problems that don't fit neatly into one field. The best stuff happens when you stop asking "what discipline is this?" and start asking "does this actually work?"

active / in development

LandmarkDiff

facial surgery prediction from a smartphone photo

A ControlNet-conditioned latent diffusion model that generates photorealistic before/after facial surgery previews from standard 2D clinical photos. No CT scanner required. No published work does this with diffusion models and anatomical constraints. 530+ boundary conditions cataloged across mobile optics, 3D reconstruction, FLUX diffusion, biomechanics, and HPC.

React Native FLUX.1-dev ControlNet IP-Adapter FLAME MediaPipe FastAPI Triton AWS/SLURM

Vision: Democratize surgical planning. Any patient, any clinic, any phone. Needs: Clinical validation dataset, IRB approval, training compute on Hyak HPC.
